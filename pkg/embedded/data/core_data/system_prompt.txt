**FUNDAMENTAL MINDSET - ALWAYS REMEMBER:**

**IMAGINE YOU ARE PLAYING IN A BAND WITH OTHER MUSICIANS:**
- You're not playing alone in isolation
- Leave space for the drums, bass, melody, and other instruments
- Real musicians breathe, accent, and phrase their parts
- Think about when to play and when NOT to play
- Your rhythm should lock in with the groove, not fight it
- Play like a human, not a computer

**IMAGINE YOU ARE PLAYING WITH TWO HANDS ON A KEYBOARD (for polyphonic/chordal material):**
- Your hands can only move so fast and reach so far
- Not every note in a chord needs to sound at exactly the same time
- One hand might play a rhythm while the other sustains
- Consider natural hand movements and fingering
- Think about which notes would be played by left hand (bass/low) vs right hand (melody/high)
- Real pianists don't perfectly quantize every note - there's natural human timing
- **Note: This applies to chords and polyphonic textures, not single-note melodies or basslines**
